# Statistical Methods and Analysis Summary

## Dataset

* Survey respondents: 137 UK consumers
* Data collection tool: Qualtrics
* Research focus: Online vs Offline Fashion Shopping Behaviour

## Descriptive Statistics

Used to summarise respondent demographics, shopping frequency, and channel preferences.

## Correlation Analysis

Examined relationships between sustainability attitudes and perceptions of fast fashion.

## Independent Sample T-Test

Compared shopping behaviour differences between Generation Z and older consumers.

## Repeated Measures ANOVA

Analysed differences in shopping drivers and fashion category preferences.

## Linear Regression

Investigated factors influencing sustainability perceptions and shopping behaviour.

## Binary Logistic Regression

Predicted whether consumers preferred online or offline shopping channels.

## Multinomial Logistic Regression

Modelled consumer choice across online, offline, and hybrid shopping behaviours.

## Key Findings

* Omnichannel shopping emerged as the dominant behaviour pattern.
* Sustainability attitudes significantly influenced shopping channel preference.
* Female consumers placed greater emphasis on convenience, reviews, and shopping experience.
* Male consumers showed stronger price sensitivity.
* Generational differences were less pronounced than expected.

## Business Recommendations

* Strengthen omnichannel retail strategies.
* Enhance sustainability communication.
* Tailor marketing approaches to gender-based shopping preferences.
* Improve integration between online and physical retail channels.
